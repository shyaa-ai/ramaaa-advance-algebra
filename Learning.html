Common Factoring Techniques

help break down algebra expressions into simpler parts, like taking something apart to understand how it’s built. You always start by looking for the Greatest Common Factor, which is what all terms share. When there are four terms, grouping them into pairs can make factoring easier. Trinomials are factored by finding number pairs or splitting the middle term. Some expressions follow quick patterns, like the difference of squares or perfect square trinomials. Overall, factoring becomes easier once you recognize patterns and simplify step by step.
